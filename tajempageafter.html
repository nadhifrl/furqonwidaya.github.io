TAJEM

Tajem is an abbreviation of Dike-Jember. The Tajem requirement was used for activities to enliven the independence day of the Republic of Indonesia where people walked 30 km from the embankment to Jember. Tajem activities are carried out in August every year. More than 10 thousand people are registered in each implementation. This editorial aims to trace Indonesian independence fighters during the colonial period who carried out guerrillas by walking tens of kilometers. Tajemtra participants get high marks if they collect stamped evidence from the six posts determined by the committee. In addition to the six assessment points, this year was added with a stamp from the mosque's youth. Head of the Youth and Sports Service, Jember M Thamrin, said that his office provides a number of facilities at each post. There are health facilities, also massages. At each post, we provide free massages. Whereas in the mosque prayer is added to prayer equipment such as mukena for female participants. Registration for Tajemtra this year is still free.
